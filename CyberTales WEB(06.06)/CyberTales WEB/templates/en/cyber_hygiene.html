Cyber Hygiene Theory Book

Chapter 1: What is Cyber Hygiene?

Cyber hygiene refers to the practices and steps users take to maintain online safety and secure digital systems. It includes everything from using antivirus software to practicing safe browsing habits.

Like personal hygiene prevents illness, cyber hygiene prevents data breaches, identity theft, malware infections, and other security incidents.

Routine behaviors such as password updates, software patching, and cautious email handling are the pillars of good cyber hygiene.

Chapter 2: Password Management

Strong passwords should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters.

Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts. Use a trusted password manager to store and generate complex passwords.

Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible to add an extra layer of protection.

Chapter 3: Software Updates

Outdated software often contains known vulnerabilities. Keeping your system, browser, and applications up to date closes these security holes.

Enable automatic updates or regularly check for new versions from official sources.

Chapter 4: Phishing Awareness

Phishing attacks attempt to trick users into giving away sensitive information through fake emails, messages, or websites.

Be skeptical of unexpected messages requesting personal data. Look for odd URLs, misspellings, or urgent language pressuring immediate action.

Never click suspicious links. Report phishing emails to your email provider or employer.

Chapter 5: Network Safety

Public Wi-Fi can be dangerous. Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on open networks without a VPN.

Secure your home Wi-Fi with a strong password and WPA3 encryption. Change your router's default admin credentials.

Disable file sharing and limit device access on shared networks.

Chapter 6: Backup and Recovery

Regularly back up your data to cloud services or external hard drives to protect against data loss from ransomware or hardware failure.

Use automated backup schedules to ensure consistency.

Test your recovery process periodically to ensure your backups are valid and usable.

Chapter 7: Safe Browsing

Stick to trusted websites. Check for HTTPS in the browser bar to ensure secure connections.

Avoid downloading from sketchy sites or clicking unknown ads. Use browser extensions like ad blockers and anti-trackers.

Clear cookies and browsing data regularly, and be mindful of website permissions.
